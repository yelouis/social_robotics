# 🤖 Social Robotics: Social-Affective Filter (SAF)

**Social Robotics** is an extensible data processing engine designed to extract social-affective metadata from large-scale egocentric (first-person) video datasets. By analyzing human reactions, gaze, and prosody in real-world interactions, we aim to build the foundational data required for the next generation of socially-aware robots.

---

## 💡 The Thesis: The Robotic Social Flywheel

At its core, this project is driven by the hypothesis that **we can build a flywheel effect for robotics by learning to interpret human emotions toward the actions a robot performs in real-time.**

1.  **Interpretation**: Developing the ability for robots to "read the room"—understanding whether a human is startled, engaged, or approving of a specific maneuver.
2.  **Reward Signals**: Human emotional responses (e.g., a flinch, a nod, or a change in vocal tone) serve as natural, high-fidelity reward signals.
3.  **Real-Time Learning**: By using these affective signals as feedback, robots can refine their trajectories and behaviors in real-time, leading to safer and more intuitive human-robot interaction (HRI).

The dataset generated by this pipeline—a massive, "dehydrated" collection of social metadata—serves as the primary training and evaluation engine for this vision.

---

## 🏗️ Pipeline Architecture

The SAF pipeline is built for scale and extensibility, processing Terabytes of video from sources like **Ego4D, EPIC-KITCHENS, Charades-Ego, and EgoProceL**.

### 1. Dataset Acquisition
Automated ingestion of raw egocentric video sources. Due to licensing, we maintain a "Download-Filter-Discard" strategy, only persisting what is necessary for metadata extraction.

### 2. Filtering & Task Labeling
We strictly filter for "Socially Relevant" moments:
-   **Social Presence**: Videos must contain at least one bystander (excluding the POV actor).
-   **Task Context**: Videos are labeled by the task being performed (using VLMs like Qwen2.5-VL and Moondream) and centered around a "Task Climax"—the moment of highest kinetic or cognitive intensity.

### 3. Social Feature Layers
The heart of the project. Independent modules analyze the filtered video chunks to extract specific social signals:

| Layer ID | Name | Focus | Model / Technique |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **03a** | **Attention** | Gaze tracking & engagement scores | L2CS-Net |
| **03b** | **Reasonable Emotion** | Context-aware emotional analysis | Py-Feat / Gemma 4 |
| **03c** | **Acoustic Prosody** | Non-verbal vocal cues & tone | emotion2vec+ / SenseVoice |
| **03d** | **Proxemic Kinematics** | Relative depth & physical spacing | Depth Anything V2 |
| **03f** | **Motor Resonance** | Sympathetic flinch & startle detection | RTMPose |

### 4. Dehydrated Export
The final output is a **Dehydrated Social Metadata Dataset**. We never export raw pixels. Instead, we provide:
-   **Identifying Metadata**: Rehydration instructions for original video sources.
-   **Social Signals**: The exact time-series data of gaze, prosody, and emotion derived by our layers.
